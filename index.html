How socioeconomic class effects education for minorities/POC

Education disparities are a severe issue that applies to millions of people to this day and will continue to have long-lasting and generational effects. It is important to spread awareness to minimize the disproportionate learning losses for low-income students of color.

Children Left Behind

Children born into low socioeconomic households can commonly be “left behind” in schools. It is not uncommon for a child to fall behind in a subject or specific class. However, when this happens it takes extra time, attention, effort, and resources to bring that child back to where they need to be. Also, some children have natural learning deficiencies, such as a deficiency in reading, speech, or math. This usually requires specialized help with the area they struggle in and in some cases their overall learning. In these cases, if the child's school does not offer programs to support and help the child, then the child will have to seek outside help. However, some parents can't afford to get their children this extra support or can't afford to move their children to a school with these programs already implemented. Without the proper help, these children will most likely struggle very early on in school and fall far behind. There is no way around helping these struggling children, the only way to support them is to make the specialized help they need affordable and available. However, this is an issue that can be solved. Schools, especially those in low socioeconomic areas, need increased funding for these specialized support programs to exist. By supporting increased funding for schools we can give every child the help and support they need.
